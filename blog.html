What is Grassroots AI?

July 18, 2016

There is a lot of hype surrounding AI (artificial intelligence) right now. As usual there are the dreamers who believe that machine-assisted utopia is around the corner, and the alarmists who believe in a Terminator armageddon where machines take over the world. AI is just another technology. Like the invention of the "wheel" a few thousand years ago, humankind can use it for transport or as an instrument of torture.

There is a common overlap of reference between artificial intelligence and machine learning. Machine learning used to be called data mining - the algorithm "learns" what is a cat, not necessarily by a descriptive formula that identifies object = cat, but by processing millions of positive examples of a picture of a cat and finding "patterns" in the data that outputs a probabilistic statement about the likelihood of that object being a cat.

Machine learning is trending toward becoming a commodity. Google offers its basic algorithm and infrastructure as a self-service tool. Most ML algorithms currently in production are standard. The factor that distinguish the Googles and the Facebook Machine Learning projects from an amateur's is only the scale of the data and the infrastructure needed to process the tetrabytes of queries per second. This is precisely what is creating a trend toward Big Data and what is making it increasingly an oligarchy of corporations who have the billions of dollars to buy servers and super infrastructure engineers like Jeff Dean who invent new ways to process even more bytes of data online.

AI from Small Data

But that is going to lead to a world where artificial intelligence will be in the province of corporations - and the profit motive. Google will develop AI that will get people to click on search ads, and Facebook will unleash AI on getting people to install more apps from their news feed. How can we make AI more personal, and more tractable?

Games are the Key

Gorilla AI's mission is to develop massively multi-player online games (MMOGs) that harnesses the intelligence of the crowd so that all the members playing the game learn and benefit from it. There is no single harvester of data or single repository where it can be exploited or stolen. Each person/player contributes to the whole, just like in MMOGs.
